About Project

This project was created to show the linguistic and cultural diversity of London. Behind every language stands a history, a rich tapestry of ideas and traditions that result in the communities we see today, with London acting as a microcosm of these effects. Our data comes from the 2011 Population Census of the UK, wherein respondents noted what language was the main language spoken at home. The language second in popularity after English was taken as representative of the area, and so we arrive at our map. We hope you find it entertaining and thought-provoking to learn more about the most prevalent language after English in your area, perhaps even helpful to find the kinds of food, shops or events that you are looking for!

This website is a result of our studies at Code First:Girls, a Not For Profit on a mission to teach 20,000 women to code by 2020.

Disclaimer

As of time of writing, the 2011 Census data is 8 years old. Some things are bound to have changed since then: population numbers growing and shrinking, communities changing, even the connectivity and size of the city itself. The data itself may be imperfect – collecting information from about 56 million people is no small task, and if a household didn’t respond, then it’s linguistic information could not have been collected (to learn more about the census history and methodology click here). Even if we pretend that the data is perfect and time doesn’t move forward, synonymising language and culture, or even more broadly language and country or language and ethnicity has its limitations: a single country may be home to dozens, if not hundreds of languages, cultures and ethnicities, and languages may cross country borders. The nature of the exercise is such that some communities may not be represented and some may appear over-represented. We sincerely wish not to offend anyone by this.
